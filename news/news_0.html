Ferrari is the first team to evaluate the MadRing circuit

11 July 2026 By F1 Editorial

Ferrari became the first Formula 1 team whose car drove 200 km on the freshly laid asphalt of the new MadRing circuit – yesterday we told you that the Scuderia was holding an official filming day there.

According to journalists from the Spanish newspaper Marca, despite the 39-degree heat, hundreds of fans gathered near the IFEMA complex, who wanted to be present at this historic event and capture it.

The day was divided into two sessions. In the morning, Charles Leclerc drove several laps without any problems, completing a total of 16 laps. However, there were some difficulties with the filming equipment, but the track continued to operate.

In addition, the Italian team was the first to test the pit lane at the Madrid circuit, and they were quite pleased with the layout. When the construction of the circuit, which is set to become the permanent home of the Spanish Grand Prix starting in September 2026, began, there were concerns about whether it would be completed on time. However, it can now be said that the circuit has undergone a baptism of fire, and Ferrari has demonstrated that it is ready for racing.

Work on the circuit is in its final stages, and the construction of the grandstands is proceeding at a rapid pace. One of the features of the circuit was the tunnel, after which the track enters the main straight of the circle, and on his last lap Leclair slowed down there to wave to the assembled public.

In the afternoon, Charles was replaced by Lewis Hamilton, and in total, the Ferrari drivers drove 36 laps along the 5.4 km long track.
