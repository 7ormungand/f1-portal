Lando Norris is ready to compete in Le Mans with Rossi

11 July 2026 By F1 Editorial

We've noted many times that Lando Norris has a long-standing friendship with Valentino Rossi, a nine-time world motorcycle champion. The 47-year-old began his career in endurance racing, and today, the crowd gathered at Goodwood, England, for the Festival of Speed ​​had the rare opportunity to see both champions at once.

Only the most important guests are invited to the balcony of the mansion of Charles Gordon-Lennox, the 11th Duke of Richmond, founder of the world-famous motorsport festival, and today that honor fell to Norris and Rossi.

Karun Chandhok, a former Formula 1 driver and current Sky Sports commentator, was chatting with Lando there. He asked, among other things, how big they were, that they were teammates and would compete together in the 24 Hours of Le Mans, since the McLaren factory team will debut in the Hypercar category of the World Endurance Championship next year.

"We have too many races in Formula 1, but since McLaren is already ready for Le Mans, maybe not next year, but in two or three years, I would love to," Lando replied. "It would be a great honor for me, and it would be really interesting!"

"Great! Then I'll try to wait for Lando!" Valentino added. "But I've been following Formula 1 since the '90s, because I'm a bit older." I'm lucky, I know Lando and the other riders, and I'm in the paddock because Stefano Domenicali is my friend. (laughs)

It's true that modern Formula 1 isn't always easy to understand, but it's still a great show, because a lot happens on the track, and we see a lot of overtaking."

When Karun asked Lando if he'd ever consider trying motorcycle racing, the reigning Formula 1 champion replied, "Actually, before karting, I started racing motorcycles, but I had an accident, so that wasn't necessarily the case. But I've already started thinking about building a MotoGP motorcycle—I just don't tell Zak Brown!"

Hearing this, Rossi advised him to start with a less powerful and more accessible category and look at the Superbike class.

"Of course, right now I'm completely focused on Formula 1," Lando asserts. "I'll try to win more championships, but life is short and time flies, so I want to get back on a motorcycle and experience what motorcycle racing is all about."

Naturally, Rossi immediately invited Norris to come and study at his VR46 racing academy.
